## ‚öÝÔ∏è Two paper-fidelity caveats (kept ‚Äî these are facts about the paper)

- **Hopf-fibration / carry provenance.** The private `hopf_fibration` namespace
  reserves room for Dudzik's transcript-only conjecture (modular-arithmetic carry
  as an S¬π-fibration of S¬≥ ‚Üí S¬≤). This is **not** in the published CDL paper, nor
  in the Filter Equivariants follow-up ([arXiv:2507.08796](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.08796),
  ¬ß6 puts ripple-carry outside its framework). The module stays private and is
  **not** audited here.
- **App H worked-example arithmetic ‚Äî reconciled against the PDF.** The paper's
  H.1/H.3 **mix matrix conventions**: `W` is *displayed* as `[[w‚ÇÅ,w‚ÇÉ],[w‚ÇÇ,w‚ÇÑ]]`
  (column-major) but eqs. (11)/(12) do the arithmetic *row-major* (as if
  `W = [[w‚ÇÅ,w‚ÇÇ],[w‚ÇÉ,w‚ÇÑ]]`). Read one convention consistently and the paper's
  stated results follow and are internally correct ‚Äî H.1's equivariance ‚Üí
  symmetric `[[w‚ÇÅ,w‚ÇÇ],[w‚ÇÇ,w‚ÇÅ]]`; H.3's invariance ‚Üí shared-*rows*
  `[[w‚ÇÅ,w‚ÇÉ],[w‚ÇÅ,w‚ÇÉ]]` (the paper says "the matrix has shared rows"). But the
  display-vs-arithmetic mismatch is a genuine transcription trap: a clean
  single-(column-major)-convention re-derivation of H.3 gives shared-*columns*
  instead. So re-derive the intermediate matrix under your own convention before
  citing it. The structural conclusions (equivariance ‚áí circulant ‚áí CNN;
  invariance ‚áí weight sharing) are convention-independent and correct, and are
  what the `Z2Group` GDL test exercises ‚Äî the test does **not** depend on the
  intermediate arithmetic. (An earlier version of this note called the flag a
  third-party artifact; that over-corrected ‚Äî the trap is real, it's just a
  notation inconsistency, not a computational error.)
