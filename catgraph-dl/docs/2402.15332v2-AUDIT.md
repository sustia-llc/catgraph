## ‚ö†ÔłŹ Two paper-fidelity caveats (kept ‚ÄĒ these are facts about the paper)

- **Hopf-fibration / carry provenance.** The private `hopf_fibration` namespace
  reserves room for Dudzik's transcript-only conjecture (modular-arithmetic carry
  as an S¬Ļ-fibration of S¬≥ ‚Üí S¬≤). This is **not** in the published CDL paper, nor
  in the Filter Equivariants follow-up ([arXiv:2507.08796](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.08796),
  ¬ß6 puts ripple-carry outside its framework). The module stays private and is
  **not** audited here.
- **App H worked-example arithmetic ‚ÄĒ reconciled against the PDF.** An earlier
  version of this audit flagged H.1/H.3 as internally inconsistent (a
  "columns-vs-rows" discrepancy). That flag came from a **third-party merged
  rendering, not the published PDF** ‚ÄĒ the PDF's derivations are self-consistent:
  H.1's equivariance forces the *symmetric* matrix `[[w‚āĀ,w‚āā],[w‚āā,w‚āĀ]]`, and H.3's
  invariance forces the *row-shared* `[[w‚āĀ,w‚āĀ],[w‚āÉ,w‚āÉ]]` (the paper itself says
  "the matrix has shared rows"). The one genuine trap is that `W` is written
  **column-major** (`W = [[w‚āĀ,w‚āÉ],[w‚āā,w‚āĄ]]`), so eq. (11)'s index bookkeeping is
  easy to mis-transcribe ‚ÄĒ re-derive from your own convention before quoting an
  intermediate step. The structural conclusions (equivariance ‚áí circulant ‚áí CNN;
  invariance ‚áí shared weights) are correct and are what the `Z2Group` GDL test
  exercises; the test does **not** depend on the intermediate arithmetic.
