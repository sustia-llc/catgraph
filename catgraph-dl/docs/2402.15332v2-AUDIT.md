## ⚠️ Two paper-fidelity caveats (kept — these are facts about the paper)

- **Hopf-fibration / carry provenance.** The private `hopf_fibration` namespace
  reserves room for Dudzik's transcript-only conjecture (modular-arithmetic carry
  as an S¹-fibration of S³ → S²). This is **not** in the published CDL paper, nor
  in the Filter Equivariants follow-up ([arXiv:2507.08796](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.08796),
  §6 puts ripple-carry outside its framework). The module stays private and is
  **not** audited here.
- **App H worked-example arithmetic — reconciled against the PDF.** The paper's
  H.1/H.3 **mix matrix conventions**: `W` is *displayed* as `[[w₁,w₃],[w₂,w₄]]`
  (column-major) but eqs. (11)/(12) do the arithmetic *row-major* (as if
  `W = [[w₁,w₂],[w₃,w₄]]`). Read one convention consistently and the paper's
  stated results follow and are internally correct — H.1's equivariance →
  symmetric `[[w₁,w₂],[w₂,w₁]]`; H.3's invariance → shared-*rows*
  `[[w₁,w₃],[w₁,w₃]]` (the paper says "the matrix has shared rows"). But the
  display-vs-arithmetic mismatch is a genuine transcription trap: a clean
  single-(column-major)-convention re-derivation of H.3 gives shared-*columns*
  instead. So re-derive the intermediate matrix under your own convention before
  citing it. The structural conclusions (equivariance ⇒ circulant ⇒ CNN;
  invariance ⇒ weight sharing) are convention-independent and correct, and are
  what the `Z2Group` GDL test exercises — the test does **not** depend on the
  intermediate arithmetic. (An earlier version of this note called the flag a
  third-party artifact; that over-corrected — the trap is real, it's just a
  notation inconsistency, not a computational error.)
